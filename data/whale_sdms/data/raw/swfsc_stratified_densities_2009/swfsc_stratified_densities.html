Title:
Predictive Modeling of Cetacean Densities in the Eastern Pacific Ocean
Description:
Abstract:
Habitat-based cetacean density models have been developed for the summer and fall period for many cetacean species in U.S. Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) waters off the U.S. West Coast (California Current Ecosystem, CCE). For other species and seasons, and in U.S. waters surrounding the Hawaiian Islands, the available data sets were not adequate to develop habitat-based models. To provide the best available density estimates for these data-limited cases, we have compiled stratified estimates of cetacean density from previously completed traditional line-transect analyses, including cold-season estimates from aerial surveys off California, harbor porpoise and coastal bottlenose dolphin density estimates from species-specific aerial surveys, density estimates from shipboard line-transect surveys in the US EEZ around Hawaii, and estimates for rarely seen species found in the CCE during 1991-2005 shipboard surveys.
Purpose:
The Navy and other users of the marine environment conduct many activities that can potentially harm marine mammals. Consequently, these entities are required to complete Environmental Assessments and Environmental Impact Statements to determine the likely impact of their activities. Specifically, those documents require an estimate of the number of animals that might be harmed or disturbed. A key element of this estimation is knowledge of cetacean (whale, dolphin, and porpoise) densities in specific areas where those activities will occur.
